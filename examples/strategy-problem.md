# Example: Unclear Product Strategy

## User Problem

The team has too many roadmap ideas. Some stakeholders want enterprise features, others want PLG improvements, and others want AI features. The roadmap feels like a collection of requests rather than a strategy.

## Diagnosis

- Primary problem type: Product Strategy.
- Secondary problem type: Prioritization / Alignment.
- Dominant uncertainty: Alignment and market.
- Decision needed: what strategic direction should organize roadmap choices.
- Recommended posture: decide and align.

## Recommended Framework

Primary framework: Strategy Kernel.

Supporting frameworks:

- Product Strategy Stack to connect strategy to roadmap.
- Weighted Decision Matrix or RICE only after the guiding policy is clear.

Alternatives considered:

- RICE is premature because the core disagreement is strategic, not scoring.
- OKRs are premature until the team agrees on diagnosis and guiding policy.
- Wardley Mapping may help if the strategic choice depends on ecosystem evolution or build/buy decisions.

## Working Canvas

### Diagnosis

What is the core challenge? Examples:

- The product is split between enterprise and self-serve motions.
- The team lacks a clear target customer.
- AI work is being treated as a feature trend rather than a strategic capability.
- Stakeholders are optimizing for different success definitions.

### Guiding Policy

What policy would focus choices? Examples:

- Win a specific ICP before broadening.
- Improve self-serve activation before adding enterprise complexity.
- Use AI only where it strengthens the core workflow, not as a parallel product.

## Point Of View

The team needs a strategy before a prioritization model. Once the guiding policy is explicit, scoring tools can help sequence work. Without that, scoring will disguise unresolved disagreement.

## Next Actions

1. Write a one-page strategy kernel with diagnosis, guiding policy, and coherent actions.
2. Identify which roadmap items do not fit the guiding policy.
3. Translate the strategy into a small number of OKRs or roadmap themes.
4. Use RICE or a decision matrix only within the chosen strategic lane.
