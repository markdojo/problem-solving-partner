# Example: Positioning Confusion

## User Problem

Customers seem interested in demos, but sales calls stall because prospects compare us to generic project management tools. The team believes the product is more strategic than that, but the market does not seem to understand the difference.

## Diagnosis

- Primary problem type: Positioning / Market Narrative.
- Secondary problem type: Sales-Led Growth Motion.
- Dominant uncertainty: Market and value.
- Decision needed: how to frame the product so the right customers understand why it matters.
- Recommended posture: decide and explain.

## Recommended Framework

Primary framework: April Dunford Positioning.

Supporting frameworks:

- Competitive Alternatives to identify what customers would use instead.
- ICP Segmentation to choose the customer context where the value is sharpest.

Alternatives considered:

- JTBD can help understand customer progress, but the immediate issue is market framing.
- Category Design may help later if the product truly requires a new category.

## Working Canvas

### Competitive Alternatives

What would customers use if this product did not exist?

### Unique Attributes

What capabilities or attributes are meaningfully different from those alternatives?

### Value

What value do those attributes enable for the customer?

### Best-Fit Customers

Which customers care most urgently about that value?

### Market Category

What context helps customers quickly understand the product?

### Relevant Trends

What changes make this product more important now?

## Point Of View

The product should not be framed against the broad project management category if the strongest value is strategic coordination, executive visibility, or operating rhythm. The team should identify the alternative that best-fit customers actually use today and position against that.

## Next Actions

1. Interview recent won, lost, and stalled prospects about alternatives.
2. Draft positioning with alternatives, attributes, value, segment, category, and trends.
3. Test the new positioning in sales calls and landing page copy.
