# Problem Taxonomy

Use this taxonomy to diagnose the problem before recommending a framework. A problem can have a primary type and one or two secondary types.

## Intake Fields

Capture these when available:

- **Problem statement**: what feels stuck, broken, unclear, or worth improving.
- **Decision needed**: what the user needs to decide, design, learn, or align.
- **User/customer**: who experiences the problem or buys the solution.
- **Business context**: stage, model, market, growth motion, and constraints.
- **Evidence**: data, research, anecdotes, stakeholder beliefs, and known gaps.
- **Stakes**: impact, urgency, reversibility, and risk.
- **Time horizon**: hours, days, weeks, quarters, or years.
- **Output desired**: brief, canvas, roadmap, experiment, narrative, decision, or facilitation plan.

## Primary Problem Types

### 1. Problem Discovery
- **Question**: What customer problem should we solve?
- **Signals**: unclear user pain, many anecdotes, low confidence, early-stage idea, solution-first thinking.
- **Likely frameworks**: JTBD, Opportunity Solution Tree, Assumption Mapping, Research Plan, Customer Journey Map.

### 2. Solution Discovery
- **Question**: Which solution approach should we try?
- **Signals**: problem is known, multiple possible solutions, uncertainty about usability or value.
- **Likely frameworks**: Design Sprint, Crazy 8s, Riskiest Assumption Test, Prototype/Test Plan, Value Proposition Canvas.

### 3. UX / Experience Improvement
- **Question**: How do we improve a user flow or experience?
- **Signals**: friction, drop-off, confusion, support tickets, usability concerns, poor task completion.
- **Likely frameworks**: Journey Map, Task Analysis, Cognitive Walkthrough, Heuristic Evaluation, HEART, Service Blueprint.

### 4. Product Strategy
- **Question**: What should the product become and where should we focus?
- **Signals**: roadmap sprawl, unclear bets, competing directions, weak alignment, vague strategy.
- **Likely frameworks**: Strategy Kernel, Product Strategy Stack, North Star Framework, Wardley Mapping, Playing To Win, OKRs.

### 5. Growth Strategy
- **Question**: How should this product grow?
- **Signals**: acquisition plateaus, weak activation, retention issues, unclear channel strategy, growth team confusion.
- **Likely frameworks**: Growth Loops, AARRR/AAARRR, Growth Model, Growth Accounting, Activation Framework, Lifecycle Marketing Map.

### 6. Retention / Engagement
- **Question**: Why do users not keep using or deepening usage?
- **Signals**: churn, low repeat usage, weak habits, poor activation-to-retention conversion, unclear core value.
- **Likely frameworks**: Retention Curve Analysis, Engagement Loop, Hook Model, JTBD, Customer Journey Map, HEART.

### 7. Monetization / Packaging
- **Question**: How should value be captured?
- **Signals**: pricing uncertainty, low conversion to paid, packaging confusion, expansion weakness, revenue leakage.
- **Likely frameworks**: Monetization Ladder, Value Metric Analysis, Subscription Value Loop, Business Model Canvas, Willingness-To-Pay Research.

### 8. Positioning / Market Narrative
- **Question**: How should customers understand and choose this?
- **Signals**: weak messaging, category confusion, sales objections, undifferentiated claims, unclear ICP.
- **Likely frameworks**: April Dunford Positioning, Geoffrey Moore Positioning, Competitive Alternatives, Category Design, ICP Segmentation.

### 9. Prioritization
- **Question**: What should we do first?
- **Signals**: too many ideas, limited resources, stakeholder conflict, roadmap pressure, unclear tradeoffs.
- **Likely frameworks**: RICE, ICE, Weighted Decision Matrix, Cost Of Delay/WSJF, MoSCoW, One-Way/Two-Way Door.

### 10. Strategic Landscape / Competition
- **Question**: What is changing around us and how should we respond?
- **Signals**: market shifts, competitor moves, platform changes, ecosystem uncertainty, build/buy/partner questions.
- **Likely frameworks**: Wardley Mapping, Porter Five Forces, PESTLE, SWOT, Blue Ocean, Ansoff Matrix.

### 11. Systems / Complexity
- **Question**: Why does the system keep producing this outcome?
- **Signals**: recurring problems, unintended consequences, many actors, incentives, feedback loops, organizational drag.
- **Likely frameworks**: Systems Map, Causal Loop Diagram, Theory Of Constraints, Iceberg Model, Leverage Points.

### 12. Execution / Operating Model
- **Question**: How should we organize to make progress?
- **Signals**: unclear ownership, slow decisions, handoff issues, cross-functional tension, planning breakdowns.
- **Likely frameworks**: DACI/RAPID, RACI, Theory Of Constraints, OKRs, Narrative Strategy Memo, Premortem.

### 13. Ambiguous Strategic Decision
- **Question**: What is the best path when the problem itself is fuzzy?
- **Signals**: multiple interpretations, high stakes, limited evidence, mixed stakeholder goals, anxiety about making the wrong call.
- **Likely frameworks**: Strategy Kernel, Decision Journal, Assumption Mapping, Weighted Decision Matrix, Narrative Strategy Memo, OODA.

## Uncertainty Types

Select the dominant uncertainty. This often matters more than the surface topic.

- **Customer uncertainty**: who has the problem, why it matters, and what progress they seek.
- **Value uncertainty**: whether the proposed solution creates meaningful value.
- **Usability uncertainty**: whether people can understand and use the solution.
- **Viability uncertainty**: whether the business model, cost, pricing, or channel works.
- **Feasibility uncertainty**: whether the team can build, operate, or deliver it.
- **Growth uncertainty**: whether adoption, retention, referrals, or monetization can compound.
- **Market uncertainty**: whether category, timing, competition, or external forces support the move.
- **Alignment uncertainty**: whether stakeholders agree on goals, ownership, and tradeoffs.
- **System uncertainty**: whether feedback loops and incentives will undermine the intervention.

## Diagnostic Questions

Ask only the questions needed. Do not turn every conversation into a survey.

### Fast Diagnosis
1. What decision or output do you need from this work?
2. Who is the customer, user, buyer, or stakeholder affected?
3. What evidence do you already have, and what is just belief?
4. What would happen if you made no decision for 30 days?
5. Is this mostly a customer, product, growth, market, system, or alignment problem?

### When The User Is Solution-First
1. What customer struggle or business outcome led to this solution?
2. What alternatives would users use if this did not exist?
3. What must be true for this solution to work?
4. What is the fastest way to learn whether that is true?

### When The User Wants Growth
1. Is the main constraint acquisition, activation, retention, referral, monetization, or expansion?
2. What is the product's repeatable value event?
3. Where does output from the product feed future input, if anywhere?
4. Which segment retains best, grows fastest, or pays most willingly?

### When The User Wants Strategy
1. What are the competing choices?
2. What tradeoff is the team avoiding?
3. What would winning look like in one year?
4. What capabilities or constraints make this team different?

### When The User Wants UX Help
1. Which user, flow, or task is failing?
2. Where does the user first experience confusion or friction?
3. What behavior or metric tells us the experience is better?
4. Are the root causes interface, information, motivation, trust, or operations?

### When The User Wants Prioritization
1. What objective are we optimizing for?
2. What constraints are real?
3. Which options are reversible?
4. What evidence would change the ranking?
